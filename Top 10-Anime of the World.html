Anime of the World

Anime, the distinctive style of Japanese animation, has evolved far beyond its cultural roots to become a powerful global art form that captivates audiences of all ages. Unlike traditional Western cartoons, anime often combines visually striking artwork with complex storytelling, exploring profound emotional and philosophical themes. Its ability to blend fantasy with realism allows creators to tell stories that resonate deeply with viewers—whether through the futuristic landscapes of Ghost in the Shell, the heartfelt coming-of-age journey in Your Lie in April, or the epic battles of Naruto and One Piece. Anime’s diversity is one of its greatest strengths; it encompasses countless genres such as romance, science fiction, horror, slice-of-life, and psychological thrillers, ensuring that there is something for every kind of viewer.

Beyond entertainment, anime has become a significant cultural force influencing fashion, music, art, and even global media production. The emotional storytelling and unique aesthetic have inspired countless artists and filmmakers around the world, bridging cultural divides and introducing audiences to Japanese traditions, language, and values. Conventions, cosplay, and fan communities have created entire subcultures that celebrate this medium’s creativity and inclusivity. Whether through the quiet introspection of Studio Ghibli films or the high-octane energy of shōnen series, anime continues to expand its boundaries, proving that animation can be just as expressive and meaningful as any live-action storytelling. In essence, anime is not just a form of entertainment—it’s a living, evolving expression of imagination that connects people through shared emotion, artistry, and wonder. 1. Dragon Ball Z

Written by: Akira Toriyama

Genre: Action, Adventure, Fantasy, Martial Arts

Story Summary:

Dragon Ball Z follows the story of Son Goku, a kind-hearted and powerful warrior who protects Earth from a variety of villains ranging from intergalactic conquerors to androids and magical creatures. The series begins five years after the events of Dragon Ball, with Goku discovering his alien origins as a Saiyan — a powerful warrior race. Throughout the story, Goku trains hard, constantly pushing his limits to become stronger and protect his loved ones. Alongside his friends and family — Vegeta, Gohan, Piccolo, Krillin, Bulma, and others — he battles fierce enemies like Frieza, Cell, and Majin Buu. Through these intense battles, the series teaches us the values of perseverance, self-improvement, teamwork, and never giving up no matter how strong the opponent is. Known for its thrilling fights, emotional moments, and unforgettable transformations like the legendary Super Saiyan, Dragon Ball Z became one of the most influential anime in the world, inspiring generations of fans and creators alike.

Sales and Popularity:

Dragon Ball Z and the overall Dragon Ball franchise have achieved legendary global success. The manga series has sold over 300 million copies worldwide, ranking among the best-selling manga of all time. The anime adaptation became a massive hit in Japan and internationally, especially during the 1990s and early 2000s, helping popularize anime in Western countries. In addition to manga and anime, Dragon Ball Z’s films and games have brought in billions in revenue. The 2018 film Dragon Ball Super: Broly earned more than $120 million worldwide, while Dragon Ball Super: Super Hero (2022) grossed around $96 million. The franchise continues to generate enormous profits through video games, figures, and merchandise — with Bandai Namco reporting Dragon Ball as their top-earning IP, generating over 144 billion yen in revenue in 2025 alone. With its unmatched legacy, thrilling action, and timeless life lessons, Dragon Ball Z remains a pillar of anime culture, symbolizing strength, growth, and the power of never giving up.

2. One Piece

Written by: Eiichiro Oda

Genre: Adventure, Fantasy, Action

Story Summary:

One Piece follows the adventures of Monkey D. Luffy, a cheerful and determined young pirate who gains the ability to stretch like rubber after eating a mysterious fruit called the Gomu Gomu no Mi (Devil Fruit). Inspired by his childhood idol “Red-Haired” Shanks, Luffy sets out to become the Pirate King by finding the world’s greatest treasure — the legendary One Piece, left behind by the former Pirate King, Gol D. Roger. During his journey, Luffy forms his crew, the Straw Hat Pirates, including Zoro, Nami, Usopp, Sanji, Chopper, Robin, Franky, Brook, and Jinbe. Together, they sail through the Grand Line, facing powerful enemies, corrupt governments, and mysterious forces, teaching us about friendship, freedom, dreams, loyalty, and never giving up on our goals no matter how tough the journey becomes. The story is celebrated for its deep world-building, emotional storytelling, humor, and epic battles, making One Piece not just a tale of pirates — but a timeless lesson about courage, hope, and chasing one’s dreams.

Sales and Popularity:

One Piece has become one of the most successful manga and anime franchises of all time. The manga has sold over 578 million copies worldwide as of July 2025, making it the best-selling manga series in history. Every volume continues to sell over a million copies in Japan, proving its enduring fame. The anime films have also broken records — One Piece Film: Red (2022) earned nearly 31.9 billion yen globally and became one of Japan’s highest-grossing films, while movies like Stampede and Strong World were major box office hits. Beyond manga and films, the franchise generates huge revenue from anime, games, and merchandise. Toei Animation reported yearly earnings of about 22.2 billion yen from the One Piece anime, and Bandai Namco’s 2025 report showed over 139 billion yen in global sales from One Piece products and licensing. With its record-breaking sales, emotional storytelling, and powerful themes, One Piece stands as a global cultural phenomenon that continues to inspire fans across generations.

3. Bleach

Written by: Tite Kubo

Genre: Action, Supernatural, Fantasy, Adventure

Story Summary:

Bleach follows the story of Ichigo Kurosaki, a high school student who unexpectedly gains the powers of a Soul Reaper — a celestial being responsible for guiding lost souls to the afterlife and protecting the living world from evil spirits known as Hollows. When a Soul Reaper named Rukia Kuchiki transfers her powers to him during battle, Ichigo becomes involved in the hidden spiritual world that exists alongside the human one. As Ichigo learns to control his new powers, he is drawn into intense battles across different realms, including the Soul Society, Hueco Mundo, and Wandenreich, facing deadly enemies like Aizen, Ulquiorra, and Yhwach. Throughout his journey, Ichigo fights not only to protect his friends but also to discover his true identity and destiny. The series teaches us about courage, sacrifice, the meaning of friendship, and the strength to fight for what's right even when the odds are impossible. With its mix of deep emotions, stylish battles, and memorable characters, Bleach stands as one of the most iconic shonen anime and manga ever created.

Sales and Popularity:

Bleach achieved tremendous success worldwide, becoming one of the best-selling manga series of all time with over 140 million copies sold globally. The manga's popularity led to a long-running anime adaptation that aired from 2004 to 2012, later returning triumphantly in 2022 with Bleach: Thousand-Year Blood War, which was praised for its stunning animation and faithfulness to the manga's final arc. The franchise expanded into films, novels, and games, earning massive profits through global licensing and merchandise. Its movies — such as Bleach: Memories of Nobody and Fade to Black — performed well in Japan, while the 2018 live-action film introduced the story to new audiences. The Bleach brand continues to be a major influence in anime culture, celebrated for its stylish art, emotional storytelling, and epic sword battles.

4. Naruto

Written by: Masashi Kishimoto

Genre: Action, Adventure, Fantasy, Martial Arts

Story Summary:

Naruto tells the story of Naruto Uzumaki, a young and energetic ninja from the Hidden Leaf Village who dreams of becoming the Hokage, the strongest ninja and leader of his village. Despite being shunned by others because of the Nine-Tailed Fox (Kurama) sealed inside him, Naruto never gives up on his dream or his belief in friendship and determination. Throughout his journey, Naruto trains hard under mentors like Kakashi Hatake and Jiraiya, and forms deep bonds with teammates Sasuke Uchiha and Sakura Haruno. Together, they face dangerous missions, powerful enemies, and internal struggles that test their strength and resolve. The story teaches us the importance of perseverance, forgiveness, loyalty, and believing in oneself even when the world stands against you. As Naruto grows, he inspires others through his courage and unbreakable will, eventually achieving his dream and showing that anyone — no matter their past — can become a true hero.

Sales and Popularity:

Naruto is one of the most successful manga and anime franchises in history, with over 250 million copies sold worldwide as of 2025. The manga’s global success made it one of Shueisha’s “Big Three” alongside One Piece and Bleach. The anime adaptation, which ran from 2002 to 2017 (including Naruto: Shippuden), became a massive international hit and helped bring anime into mainstream culture across the world. The franchise expanded into movies, novels, games, and merchandise, generating billions in revenue. Popular films like The Last: Naruto the Movie and Boruto: Naruto the Movie were box-office hits, while the sequel series Boruto: Naruto Next Generations continues the legacy with Naruto’s son as the main character. With its emotional storytelling, unforgettable characters, and themes of friendship, pain, and redemption, Naruto remains a global cultural icon that inspires people to chase their dreams and never give up — no matter how hard the journey gets.

5. Attack On Titan (AOT)

Written by: Hajime Isayama

Genre: Action, Dark Fantasy, Drama, Post-Apocalyptic

Story Summary:

Attack on Titan is set in a world where humanity lives within massive walled cities to protect themselves from giant man-eating creatures known as Titans. The story follows Eren Yeager, along with his friends Mikasa Ackerman and Armin Arlert, who join the military to fight the Titans after their hometown is destroyed. As the series unfolds, Eren discovers shocking truths about the Titans, the walls, and the history of humanity itself. The story explores themes of freedom, survival, sacrifice, and the cycle of hatred, teaching us about the harsh realities of war and the cost of pursuing freedom. Attack on Titan is known for its deep plot, emotional storytelling, and incredible twists, making it one of the most powerful and thought-provoking anime ever created.

Sales and Popularity:

The Attack on Titan manga has sold over 140 million copies worldwide, making it one of the best-selling manga of all time. The anime adaptation, which aired from 2013 to 2023, was a global phenomenon and received widespread critical acclaim for its animation, music, and storytelling. Its final season became one of the most-watched anime events in history. The franchise has also earned billions in revenue from films, merchandise, and games, solidifying Attack on Titan as one of the most impactful modern anime series ever made.

6. Hunter X Hunter

Written by: Yoshihiro Togashi

Genre: Adventure, Fantasy, Action, Psychological

Story Summary:

Hunter X Hunter follows Gon Freecss, a cheerful young boy who discovers that his long-lost father, Ging Freecss, is a world-famous “Hunter” — a professional who takes on dangerous missions like tracking treasures, exploring uncharted lands, or capturing criminals. Determined to find his father, Gon sets out to become a Hunter himself. Along the way, he meets loyal friends — Killua, Kurapika, and Leorio — and together they face dangerous challenges, tournaments, and life-threatening adventures. The series teaches about friendship, courage, self-discovery, and the emotional complexity of human nature. Known for its brilliant storytelling, complex characters, and unpredictable plotlines, Hunter × Hunter is widely considered one of the smartest and most creative anime ever made.

Sales and Popularity:

Hunter X Hunter has sold over 85 million copies worldwide, earning its place among Japan’s most successful manga. The anime adaptations (1999 and 2011 versions) received global praise for their strong emotional writing and high-quality animation. Despite the author’s long hiatuses due to health issues, the series remains loved by millions of fans. Its games, films, and merchandise continue to perform well, proving the long-lasting popularity of Togashi’s masterpiece.

7. Death Note

Written by: Tsugumi Ohba Illustrated by: Takeshi Obata

Genre: Psychological Thriller, Mystery, Supernatural, Crime

Story Summary:

Death Note tells the story of Light Yagami, a brilliant high school student who discovers a mysterious notebook called the Death Note, which allows its user to kill anyone simply by writing their name in it. Believing he can create a perfect world free of crime, Light begins using the notebook to eliminate criminals — but soon finds himself hunted by the genius detective L. The series becomes a psychological battle between Light and L, full of strategy, deception, and moral conflict. Death Note teaches us about justice, power, corruption, and how absolute control can destroy even the purest intentions.

Sales and Popularity:

Death Note has sold over 30 million copies worldwide, ranking among the most successful psychological manga ever created. The anime adaptation (2006–2007) became a global hit, praised for its suspense, sharp writing, and iconic characters. The franchise expanded into live-action films, novels, musicals, and games, with worldwide popularity across multiple generations. Death Note remains one of the most influential anime of all time, known for its dark themes and mind-blowing plot twists that forever changed the way people view anime storytelling.

8. Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood

Written by: Hiromu Arakawa

Genre: Action, Adventure, Fantasy, Drama, Science Fiction

Story Summary:

Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood follows the story of two brothers, Edward and Alphonse Elric, who live in a world where alchemy — the science of transforming matter — is a powerful and respected art. After a tragic attempt to use alchemy to bring their mother back to life goes horribly wrong, Edward loses his arm and leg, and Alphonse loses his entire body, his soul bound to a suit of armor. Determined to restore their bodies, the brothers become State Alchemists and set off on a dangerous journey to find the mythical Philosopher’s Stone, a powerful object said to amplify alchemical abilities. Along the way, they uncover dark government secrets, ancient conspiracies, and the true cost of human ambition. The series teaches us about sacrifice, brotherhood, morality, and the consequences of trying to play god, showing that true strength lies in compassion and perseverance. With its deep themes, emotional storytelling, and perfectly structured plot, Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood is often regarded as one of the greatest anime ever made.

Sales and Popularity:

The Fullmetal Alchemist manga has sold over 80 million copies worldwide, making it one of the best-selling manga series of all time. The Brotherhood anime, released in 2009, received universal acclaim for its faithful adaptation, beautiful animation, and emotional depth — often ranked as the #1 anime of all time on major platforms like MyAnimeList. The franchise has also spawned successful films, novels, games, and live-action adaptations, continuing to generate massive global popularity. Its blend of action, heart, and philosophical depth has made Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood a timeless masterpiece that continues to inspire and move fans around the world.

9. Monster

Written by: Naoki Urasawa

Genre: Psychological Thriller, Mystery, Drama, Seinen

Story Summary:

Monster follows Dr. Kenzo Tenma, a brilliant Japanese brain surgeon living in Germany, whose life changes when he saves the life of a young boy who later grows up to become a dangerous serial killer. Haunted by guilt, Tenma sets out on a relentless journey across Europe to stop the boy, now known as Johan Liebert, while uncovering dark conspiracies and confronting moral dilemmas. The series teaches about morality, the value of human life, the consequences of choices, and the complex nature of good and evil. It is renowned for its gripping suspense, intelligent plotting, and deep psychological exploration.

Sales and Popularity:

The Monster manga has sold over 20 million copies worldwide and is acclaimed as one of the greatest psychological thriller manga ever written. The anime adaptation (2004–2005) received global praise for its intense storytelling, mature themes, and faithful adaptation of the manga. Monster continues to be influential in both manga and anime communities, celebrated for its intricate plot, moral complexity, and unforgettable characters.

10. Haikyuu!

Written by: Haruichi Furudate

Genre: Sports, Comedy, Drama, School

Story Summary:

Haikyuu! follows Shoyo Hinata, a passionate and determined high school student who dreams of becoming a top volleyball player despite his small stature. Inspired by a famous player known as “The Little Giant,” Hinata joins Karasuno High School’s volleyball team, where he forms a powerful partnership with his talented but prideful teammate Tobio Kageyama. Throughout the series, Hinata and his teammates face formidable opponents, overcome setbacks, and grow both as athletes and as friends. The story teaches us about teamwork, perseverance, self-improvement, and the importance of believing in yourself and your team.

Sales and Popularity:

The Haikyuu! manga has sold over 50 million copies worldwide, and the anime adaptation (2014–2020) became a major hit, praised for its energetic animation and accurate depiction of volleyball. The franchise also includes stage plays, video games, and merchandise, generating significant revenue. With its inspiring themes and memorable characters, Haikyuu! continues to motivate fans and athletes worldwide.
